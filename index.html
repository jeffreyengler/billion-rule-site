The 1:1 Billion Rule

No one should be a billion times richer than anyone else.

What Is the 1:1 Billion Rule?

The 1:1 Billion Rule is a simple idea: no one should earn or own more than one billion times what another person has. In a country where some have $0 and others have $200 billion, it’s time for a fair limit. The policy caps individual net worth and annual income at $1 billion. Anything beyond that is taxed and returned to the public.

Why It Matters

  • No one earns a billion dollars alone—they rely on public systems and shared infrastructure.
  • Extreme wealth concentration hurts democracy, opportunity, and fairness.
  • We can fund solutions like ending hunger, building affordable housing, and providing healthcare.

Where the Money Goes

  • End Hunger: Feed the 44 million Americans who are food insecure.
  • Affordable Housing: Bring rent down from 50% of income to 10%, like in Vienna.
  • Healthcare: Help fund universal care so no one goes bankrupt over medical bills.
